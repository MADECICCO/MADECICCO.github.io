About Me

Hello! I am Marcelo De Cicco, an astronomer passionate about studying the universe, time keeping and spatial bodies dynamics. My research focuses on time keeping, frequency and orbital dynamics of small bodies such as fireballs and artificial satellites, as well as the observation of celestial phenomena. I am actively involved as voluntary in EXOSS Citizen Science Project, which aims to monitor and analyze meteor activity.

Academic experience

  • DSc. in astronomy at National Observatory
  • MSc in astronomy at National Observatory
  • MSc. in public politics and Innovation at UFRJ
  • Researcher-Technologist at INMETRO, focusing on time keeping and the impacts of earth rotation on time measurement[^2^][5]
  • Coordinator of the EXOSS Citizen Science Project
  • Contributor to the CAMS/NASA (Cameras for Allsky Meteor Surveillance) network in Brazil
  • Participant in the Frontier Development Lab (FDL) with NASA and SETI, 2017 focusing on AI/ML research for space and Earth science[^1^][4]

Interests

My interests include the analysis of astronomical data, the observation of meteor showers, earth rotation physics and science communication. I enjoy sharing my knowledge about the universe with the general public through various platforms.

INMETRO Contributions

At INMETRO, I work as a Researcher-Technologist in the Directorate of Scientific and Technological Metrology (Dimci). My work involves studying time keeping, and the impact of earth rotation on time measurement, particularly focusing on the differences between astronomical and atomic time intervals. This research is crucial for understanding how human activities influence the Earth's rotation and, consequently, time measurement[^2^][5].
