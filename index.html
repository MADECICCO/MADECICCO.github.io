About Me

Hello! I am Marcelo De Cicco, an astronomer passionate about studying the universe. My research focuses on meteor showers and the observation of celestial phenomena. I am actively involved in the EXOSS Citizen Science Project, which aims to monitor and analyze meteor activity.

Experience

  • PhD in Astrophysics
  • Researcher at the National Observatory
  • Coordinator of the EXOSS Citizen Science Project
  • Contributor to the CAMS (Cameras for Allsky Meteor Surveillance) network in Brazil
  • Participant in the Frontier Development Lab (FDL) with NASA and SETI, focusing on AI/ML research for space and Earth science[^1^][4]
  • Researcher-Technologist at INMETRO, focusing on the impact of climate change on time measurement[^2^][5]

Interests

My interests include the observation of meteor showers, the analysis of astronomical data, and science communication. I enjoy sharing my knowledge about the universe with the general public through various platforms.

INMETRO Contributions

At INMETRO, I work as a Researcher-Technologist in the Directorate of Scientific and Technological Metrology (Dimci). My work involves studying the impact of climate change on time measurement, particularly focusing on the differences between astronomical and atomic time intervals. This research is crucial for understanding how human activities influence the Earth's rotation and, consequently, time measurement[^2^][5].
